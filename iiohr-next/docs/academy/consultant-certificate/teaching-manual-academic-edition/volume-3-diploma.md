## Programme overview (Diploma)

Diploma level integrates high-risk operational coordination, follow-up communication, service quality, and capstone demonstration of competency across the programme.

**Professional boundaries (summary):** no independent diagnosis, prescribing, surgical clearance, or substitution for **informed consent** by the treating clinician. Post-operative contact emphasises patient education, triage to clinicians, and escalation — not solo management of complications.

**Competency emphasis:** reliable pre-operative preparation without clearance, safe post-operative education, de-escalation in conflict, quality-improvement literacy, and holistic portfolio evidence.

* * *

## Module 11 — Pre-Operative Coordination and Operational Discipline

**Summary:** Operational excellence in pre-operative support: instructions, timing, documentation, consent-adjacent education, and no authoritative clearance.

**Learning outcomes:** Execute checklists; identify missing consent or contraindication review; escalate blockers.

**Assessment design:** Automated knowledge checks on pre-operative requirements; faculty-moderated coordination plan.

**Practical task:** Build a pre-operative pack for three personas (logistics-only emphasis).

* * *

## Module 12 — Post-Operative Follow-Up Communication

**Summary:** Scripts and protocols for expected recovery education; red-flag routing; psychological support within role bounds.

**Learning outcomes:** Avoid diagnostic closure; use clinician-directed pathways; document interactions.

**Assessment design:** Faculty-moderated call or message simulations; automated knowledge checks on red-flag lists.

**Practical task:** Post-operative FAQ with escalation triggers stated explicitly.

* * *

## Module 13 — Managing Difficult Encounters and Service Conflicts

**Summary:** Conflict de-escalation, complaints-handling adjacency, social media awareness, team stress.

**Learning outcomes:** Stabilise emotion; invoke clinical leadership when safety is at risk; document factually.

**Assessment design:** Faculty-moderated scenario responses; automated knowledge checks on policy basics.

**Practical task:** Complete the difficult-encounter after-action review template for two cases.

* * *

## Module 14 — Quality Assurance and Professional Development

**Summary:** Internal audit literacy, metrics interpretation without clinical authority, constructive feedback, personal development planning.

**Learning outcomes:** Participate in quality improvement; maintain an evidence portfolio; market qualifications ethically.

**Assessment design:** Automated knowledge checks on QI concepts; faculty-moderated improvement proposal.

**Practical task:** Draft one QI suggestion with non-clinical scope and feasibility notes.

* * *

## Module 15 — Capstone Integration and Competency Demonstration

**Summary:** Holistic demonstration: multi-module case thread, ethical defence, communication exemplars, documentation quality, reflection.

**Learning outcomes:** Evidence readiness for supervised practice; articulate limits clearly.

**Assessment design:** Capstone portfolio review; may include oral defence where the institution configures it.

**Practical task:** Capstone portfolio per the published rubric (minimum artefacts as specified in the LMS).
