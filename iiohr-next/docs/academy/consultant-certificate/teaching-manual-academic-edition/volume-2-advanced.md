## Programme overview (Advanced)

Advanced level develops **ethical judgement**, **therapeutic communication**, **non-surgical treatment education within scope**, **surgical-pathway literacy without surgical authority**, and **medico-legal documentation**.

**Professional boundaries (summary):** unchanged from Foundation — **no** independent diagnosis, prescribing, surgical planning, or **informed consent** substitution. Advanced work adds **higher-stakes communication** and **documentation** under scrutiny.

**Competency emphasis:** defensible records, **scope-safe** patient education on medications and procedures, and **disciplined escalation** at the **surgical** interface.

## Module 6 — Ethics, Statutory Scope, and Professional Registration

**Summary:** **Statutory** frameworks, registration boundaries, advertising ethics, conflicts of interest, and **duty** to **doctor-led** judgement.

**Learning outcomes:** map local law to daily practice; refuse out-of-scope requests; document ethically.

**Assessment design:** **automated knowledge checks** on rules; **faculty-moderated** ethics scenarios; **mandatory safety** items on **scope** where configured in the LMS.

**Practical task:** ethics case workbook — three scenarios with **governance-appropriate** responses.

* * *

## Module 7 — Therapeutic Communication and Challenging Conversations

**Summary:** **Communication** under distress, anger, gender-sensitive care, cultural humility, and **trauma-informed** **basics** (within role).

**Learning outcomes:** de-escalate; set **boundaries** kindly; involve **clinicians** when clinical or psychological risk exceeds support scope.

**Assessment design:** **faculty-moderated** role-play or structured scripts; supplementary **automated knowledge checks** on communication principles.

**Practical task:** script library for five difficult moments (**scope-safe** phrasing).

* * *

## Module 8 — Non-Surgical Treatment Education

**Summary:** **Patient education** on approved **non-surgical** options **without** dosing, switching, or monitoring claims that imply prescribing.

**Learning outcomes:** explain mechanisms and expectations; defer changes to prescribers; recognise adverse-event **reporting pathways** as **clinician-led**.

**Assessment design:** **automated knowledge checks** on drug classes; **faculty-moderated** patient education drafts.

**Practical task:** build two education plans (male pattern example; female pattern example) with **explicit** prescriber referral points.

* * *

## Module 9 — Surgical Pathway Literacy (Non-Operative)

**Summary:** **Orientation** to **surgical** flow: pre-operative checks as **support**, **intra-operative** roles **without** sterile authority, post-operative **education** **without** **clearance** for complications.

**Learning outcomes:** explain **consent** as **clinician-owned**; coordinate logistics; **never** substitute **surgical** judgement.

**Assessment design:** **linked summative assessment** (required for module completion in the LMS) with **automated knowledge checks** on **boundaries**; **faculty-moderated** documentation of a **mock** **surgical** day **support** plan.

**Practical task:** stepwise **non-authoritative** OR orientation checklist for patients (comfort, logistics, expectations) — **no** clinical clearance language.

* * *

## Module 10 — Documentation, Consent Literacy, and Safe Handover

**Summary:** **Medico-legal** robustness: contemporaneous notes, **consent-adjacent** explanations **without** **consent** procurement, **handover** chains, incident awareness **routes**.

**Learning outcomes:** produce **defensible** records; **flag** **consent** gaps for **clinicians**; **handover** with **closed-loop** elements where local policy requires.

**Assessment design:** **automated knowledge checks** on documentation law **basics**; **faculty-moderated** note and **handover** review.

**Practical task:** redact a poor note and rewrite to **standard**; draft **handover** for a complex case.
