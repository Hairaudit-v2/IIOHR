## Programme overview (Foundation)

The Foundation level establishes **role clarity**, **science literacy**, **presentation literacy**, **consultation structure**, and **screening-within-bounds** for **consultant and nurse** learners supporting **doctor-led** hair restoration services.

**Professional boundaries (summary):** learners **do not** diagnose, prescribe, plan surgery, adjudicate graft counts, or substitute for **informed consent** by the treating clinician. The role is **support, education, coordination, documentation, and escalation**.

**Competency emphasis:** reliable information capture, safe patient education within scope, structured consultation flow, and **appropriate escalation** when risk or uncertainty exceeds the support role.

* * *

## Compliance notices (LMS)

The Consultant Stream presents four standing notices: **competency and scope of practice**, **no independent diagnostic authority**, **surgical training boundary**, and **mandatory escalation duty**. Foundation **Modules** attach notices where thematic risk warrants (e.g. presentations and screening).

## Module 1 — Role Pressures and Professional Boundaries

**Summary:** Defines the **consultant and nurse** support role under commercial, emotional, and time pressure. Distinguishes **education** from **clinical decision-making**.

**Learning outcomes:** identify boundary violations; apply de-escalation; document within role; escalate when advice crosses into diagnosis or treatment planning.

**Assessment design:** **automated knowledge checks** (machine-scored in the LMS) plus **faculty-moderated** reflective or scenario responses where configured.

**Practical task:** boundary journal — document three realistic pressure moments and the **scope-safe** response.

* * *

## Module 2 — Hair Science Literacy for Patient Education

**Summary:** Builds **science literacy** for **patient-facing** explanation: growth phases, miniaturisation conceptually, treatment categories **without** recommending prescription changes.

**Learning outcomes:** explain mechanisms at lay level; avoid diagnostic labels as self-generated conclusions; defer medical decisions to the treating clinician.

**Assessment design:** **automated knowledge checks** on biology concepts; **faculty-moderated** explanation tasks assessing accuracy and tone.

**Practical task:** draft two patient explanations (one medical, one surgical pathway) **without** prescribing language.

* * *

## Module 3 — Recognising Common Hair Loss Presentations

**Summary:** **Pattern familiarity** for **routing and education**, not independent diagnosis. Links observation to **clinician verification**.

**Learning outcomes:** use neutral descriptive language; avoid definitive diagnosis; prompt **doctor-led** assessment when patterns are ambiguous or red flags appear.

**Assessment design:** image-based **automated knowledge checks** (if enabled) plus **faculty-moderated** structured descriptions.

**Practical task:** document five case vignettes with **observation → education → escalation** (no diagnosis field completed by the learner as authority).

* * *

## Module 4 — Consultation Structure and Information Gathering

**Summary:** **End-to-end consultation architecture**: opening, history, expectations, concerns, documentation, and **handover** to the treating clinician.

**Learning outcomes:** produce **handover-ready** notes; identify consent-adjacent topics that must remain **clinician-owned**; flag psychological distress.

**Assessment design:** **automated knowledge checks** on sequence and consent boundaries; **faculty-moderated** note review.

**Practical task:** full consultation write-up against a rubric (completeness, neutrality, escalation).

* * *

## Module 5 — Screening Within Scope

**Summary:** **Support** for **doctor-led** screening: medication history, pregnancy considerations where relevant, mental health **flags** (not counselling therapy), infection or inflammation signs requiring **clinician** review.

**Learning outcomes:** screen **without** clearing for surgery; recognise **stop** points; escalate promptly.

**Assessment design:** **automated knowledge checks** on contraindications; **faculty-moderated** triage scenarios.

**Practical task:** red-flag drill — list escalation triggers and **exact** wording for **handover** to a clinician.

* * *

## Foundation capstone orientation

**Modules 1–5** prepare learners for **Level 2**, where ethics, communication, non-surgical treatment education, the **surgical-pathway bridge** (**Module 9**), and medico-legal documentation deepen **judgement**.
