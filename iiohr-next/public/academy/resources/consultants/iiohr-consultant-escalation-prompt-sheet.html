Escalation prompt sheet — patient-facing language

Red flags, screening, follow-up, difficult encounters · Adapt names and routes to your clinic · Consultant / coordinator use only.

These prompts support professional handoffs. They do not replace protocol. When safety is in doubt, escalate earlier. Do not diagnose, prescribe, or guarantee outcomes when using these stems.

General routing

Handover “I’ve captured what you’ve told me accurately. The next step is for [doctor role] to review this — I’m not able to make that clinical decision from my role.”
Uncertainty “Because this is outside what I’m able to assess, I’m going to make sure the clinical team sees it today rather than us guessing.”

Screening & presentation concerns

Incomplete data “Before we go further, I need us to complete a few screening details so the doctor has the full picture — that’s safer than rushing ahead.”
Rapid or atypical change “What you’re describing needs a proper clinical look — I’ll document it neutrally and arrange review.”

Post-operative & follow-up

Possible complication “What you report could be serious. Please [follow emergency instruction if applicable]. I’m escalating this to the clinical team now and documenting what you said.”
No remote closure “I can’t tell from here whether that’s expected for you — the right thing is for the team to review you rather than me reassuring from a distance.”

Difficult or high-pressure situations

Consent / payment pressure “Clinical assessment and consent come before financial commitment here. I’m not going to shortcut that — let me get the right person involved.”
Distress / complaint risk “I hear that you’re upset. I’m going to document your concerns factually and make sure [clinician / manager] speaks with you — we won’t argue you down from feeling worried.”

After you speak — always

  • Note date, time, channel, patient words (key phrases), your summary action, who was notified.
  • If you promised a callback, log the deadline.
