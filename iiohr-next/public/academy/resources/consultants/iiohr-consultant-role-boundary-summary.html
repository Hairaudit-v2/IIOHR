Role boundary summary — consultant & clinical support

IIOHR Certificate in Hair Loss Consultation & Clinical Patient Coordination · Support reference · Adapt to local clinic policy.

Scope. This sheet describes typical boundaries for non-doctor patient-facing roles in doctor-led hair restoration services. It does not replace statutory scope of practice, employment contracts, or clinic governance. Diagnosis, prescribing, surgical planning, and informed consent for procedures remain with the treating clinician.

Within scope (support, coordination, education)

  • Structure the consultation arc; gather history and concerns using approved templates.
  • Explain general pathway stages and clinic process using neutral, non-guarantee language.
  • Document observations and patient report factually; prepare doctor-ready handovers.
  • Screen against agreed checklists; flag gaps, red flags, and time-sensitive concerns.
  • Coordinate scheduling, reminders, and logistics per protocol.
  • Escalate when questions require clinical judgement or when risk is unclear.

Outside scope — escalate, do not improvise

TopicSupport role
Diagnosis or disease naming as factDescribe; document; clinician interprets.
Suitability for surgery or technique choiceDefer to surgeon-led assessment.
Graft numbers, hairline design, medical clearanceDefer to clinical team.
Medications, dosing, stopping/starting drugsDefer to prescriber or surgeon per policy.
Informed consent for surgerySupport information flow only; consent is clinician-led.
Post-op complication assessmentDocument; arrange clinical review; no remote “all clear.”

Escalation — default to safety

When uncertain, or when the patient pushes for a clinical verdict, pause, document neutrally, and route to the responsible doctor or regulated clinician according to clinic protocol.
