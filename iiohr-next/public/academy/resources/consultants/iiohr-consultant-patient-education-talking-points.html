Patient education — scope-safe talking points

Non-surgical and pathway context · Doctor-led decisions · Use clinic-approved materials where they exist.

Educate broadly; defer specifically. You may outline categories of options and typical pathway sequencing in general terms. Personalised medication advice, dosing, suitability, and surgical planning are for the treating clinician or prescriber.

Principles

  • Use measured language: avoid absolute guarantees of density, timeline, or cosmetic outcome.
  • Separate what the clinic offers in principle from what is appropriate for this patient — the second is clinical.
  • When asked “what should I take?”, acknowledge the question and route to the prescriber or surgeon.
  • Document questions the patient cares about so the doctor encounter is efficient.

Example stems (adapt to your tone)

Pathway sequencing: “Many people consider a mix of approaches over time. Which options are relevant for you is something your doctor confirms after they’ve reviewed you properly.”
Medications: “I’m not able to advise on specific drugs or doses—that has to come from your prescriber or your surgeon as part of your medical review.”
Surgery interest: “I can explain how our pathway is usually organised; the medical plan itself is decided with your surgeon.”

Phrases to avoid (high risk)

  • “You’re definitely a good candidate.”
  • “You should start [named drug] at [dose].”
  • “You’ll need about X grafts.”
  • “It’s completely safe — no need to trouble the doctor.”
