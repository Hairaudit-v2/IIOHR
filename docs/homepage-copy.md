## 2. Problem section

### Section heading

The problem with traditional training

### Lead paragraph

Hair restoration training is often too short, too shallow, and too unsupported to build lasting surgical competence. Many doctors complete a brief course and are left without structured follow-up, clear feedback on outcomes, or a defined path to improvement.

### Bullet points (four)

**Short and shallow.**  
Intensive bursts with little time for consolidation, supervised practice, or integration of theory with technique.

**Doctors left alone after completion.**  
No structured mentoring, no outcome feedback, and no mechanism to measure or benchmark real-world results.

**Limited feedback loops.**  
Few ways to know whether technique is improving — and where to focus next.

**No real progression pathway.**  
The jump from “course completed” to “practising independently” is abrupt, with no staged development or audit-backed improvement.

## 3. Differentiation section

### Section heading

The IIOHR difference

### Lead paragraph

IIOHR is a structured surgeon development pathway — not a one-off short course. We combine practical FUE training, live-patient experience, advanced hair-loss theory and trichology, ongoing support, and audit-backed improvement through Follicle Intelligence, all informed by Australian surgical standards and discipline.

### Differentiators (six — use as list or short cards)

1. **Practical FUE surgical training** — Hands-on technique development with supervised practice and progression.
2. **Live-patient surgical exposure** — Real surgery in a controlled setting; observation and participation, not simulation alone.
3. **Advanced hair-loss theory and trichology** — Deeper understanding of biology, diagnosis, and planning so technique is informed by science.
4. **Ongoing support and mentoring** — Structured follow-up after training so development continues, not ends, at course completion.
5. **Audit-backed improvement** — Longitudinal case review and feedback via Follicle Intelligence; measurable progression, not assumed competence.
6. **Australian standards framework** — Surgical discipline and rigour that inform the curriculum and expectations — globally relevant, not parochial.
