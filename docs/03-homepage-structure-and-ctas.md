# IIOHR Website Strategy — Homepage Structure & CTAs

## Suggested Homepage Section Structure

**Scroll order:** top → bottom. One clear narrative: What is IIOHR → Why different → What we teach → How it works → Who it’s for → Who delivers it → Next step.

---

### 1. Hero
- **Headline:** Beyond the short course. From technician to surgeon.
- **Subhead:** Training. Auditing. Mentoring. Support. The pathway from course completion to true surgical mastery.
- **Visual:** Calm, premium, clinical — e.g. restrained imagery (theatre, hands, or abstract), no stock “graduation” cliché.
- **CTA:** Apply / Enquire (primary); Learn about our pathway (secondary, scroll or link to Training Pathways).

---

### 2. What is IIOHR (Intro)
- One or two short paragraphs: International Institute of Hair Restoration; premium training and standards body; advancing hair restoration surgeons beyond basic short-course education.
- Optional short bullet: live-patient FUE, advanced theory, trichology, auditing, mentoring, Australian framework.
- **CTA:** About IIOHR.

---

### 3. Why We’re Different (Differentiators)
- Section title e.g. “Not a short course. A pathway.”
- Four to six differentiators, each with a short line of copy:
  - Practical live-patient FUE training
  - Advanced hair loss science and trichology
  - Structured post-training support and mentoring
  - Longitudinal auditing with Follicle Intelligence
  - Australian surgery standards and discipline
  - Measured improvement over time — pathway to excellence
- Tone: factual, no hype. One line per differentiator; optional small supporting sentence.
- **CTA:** Training Pathways (or “See the pathway”).

---

### 4. The Pathway (Overview)
- Visual: simple stepped or timeline view of the journey (e.g. Training → Mentoring → Auditing → Ongoing support).
- Short copy: how the pathway works; from training through to continued development.
- Link to Surgeon Development Pathway and Follicle Intelligence for detail.
- **CTA:** Explore the Surgeon Development Pathway; Follicle Intelligence Integration.

---

### 5. What We Teach (Curriculum Snapshot)
- Two pillars (or three):
  - **Practical:** Live-patient FUE; hands-on surgery; supervised practice.
  - **Theory:** Hair loss biology; trichology; advanced science.
  - **Development:** Auditing; mentoring; measurable improvement.
- One or two sentences per pillar; no course syllabus dump.
- **CTA:** Practical FUE Training; Hair Loss Science and Trichology.

---

### 6. Follicle Intelligence (Integration Teaser)
- One short block: how Follicle Intelligence supports post-training development; longitudinal auditing; data-led improvement.
- No deep technical detail here — “see Follicle Intelligence Integration” for more.
- **CTA:** Follicle Intelligence Integration.

---

### 7. Who It’s For (Audience)
- Title e.g. “Who the pathway is for.”
- Short list or cards: doctors entering hair restoration; existing surgeons raising outcomes; clinics; international doctors; those wanting deeper trichology/science; those frustrated by low-support training.
- One line per audience; inclusive, not exclusive.
- **CTA:** Apply / Enquire; For Clinics (if clinic audience is highlighted).

---

### 8. Standards (Australian Framework Teaser)
- One short paragraph: Australian surgery standards and discipline inform the program; global relevance.
- Avoid regulatory or legal overclaim.
- **CTA:** Standards and Australian Framework.

---

### 9. Faculty / Mentors (Trust)
- Short section: experienced faculty and mentors; credibility of the pathway.
- Option: two or three names or roles with one-line descriptors; or single line “Meet our faculty” with link.
- **CTA:** Faculty / Mentors.

---

### 10. Why This Works (Outcomes Teaser)
- One short block: measured improvement; audit-led development; why training + mentoring + auditing works.
- Frame as rationale, not guarantee.
- **CTA:** Outcomes / Why This Works.

---

### 11. Final CTA Block
- Headline e.g. “Begin the pathway” or “Apply or enquire.”
- One sentence: who should apply and what the next step is.
- **CTA:** Apply / Enquire (dominant).

---

### 12. Footer
- Links: About, Training Pathways (and key sub-pages), Standards, For Clinics, Faculty, Outcomes, Apply / Enquire.
- Optional: short line about IIOHR (e.g. “International Institute of Hair Restoration — training, auditing, mentoring, support.”).
- Legal: Privacy, Terms if needed.

---

## Core CTAs (Site-Wide)

| CTA | Placement | Purpose |
|-----|-----------|--------|
| **Apply / Enquire** | Header (persistent), Hero, end of key sections, final homepage block, Apply page | Primary conversion; serious interest. |
| **Training Pathways** / **See the pathway** | Home, About, For Clinics, footer | Move into curriculum and structure. |
| **Explore [specific page]** | Home (pathway, FUE, trichology, Follicle Intelligence), inner pages | Deep-dive into one topic. |
| **About IIOHR** | Home, footer | Identity and credibility. |
| **For Clinics** | Home (who it’s for), footer | Clinic-specific entry. |
| **Faculty / Mentors** | Home, footer | Trust and people. |
| **Outcomes / Why This Works** | Home, Follicle Intelligence, Surgeon Development | Rationale and evidence. |

**Principles:**
- One primary CTA per page: Apply / Enquire where conversion matters; otherwise the main next step (e.g. “See the pathway”).
- Secondary CTAs support journey: pathway pages, About, Faculty, Outcomes.
- No “Buy now” or “Enrol today” — use “Apply” or “Enquire” to keep tone premium and considered.
