Training Pathways

Practical FUE training

Hands-on FUE training with live-patient surgical exposure under supervision. Not simulation alone — real surgery in a controlled setting, as part of a structured surgeon development pathway.

Why practical FUE matters

Technique is built through repetition, feedback, and supervised practice — not through theory or simulation alone.

IIOHR’s practical FUE component is designed to take you from principles to performance. You learn extraction, handling, and placement in a structured way; you observe live surgery; and you perform under supervision with real patients. The focus is on skill progression, safety, and standards — so that when you leave the intensive training phase, you have a foundation that can be refined through ongoing mentoring and audit-backed feedback.

What the practical pathway covers

A progression from observation to supervised performance.

FUE principles and technique

Systematic coverage of extraction, graft handling, incision and placement, and planning — aligned with Australian standards and best practice.

Observation and immersion

Live-patient surgery in a controlled environment. You observe decision-making, workflow, and technique in real time before hands-on involvement.

Supervised hands-on practice

Performance with live patients under direct supervision. Immediate feedback and refinement so technique improves with each case.

How it fits the full pathway

Practical FUE is one pillar of the IIOHR pathway — integrated with theory, auditing, and ongoing support.

Hands-on training is reinforced by advanced hair-loss science and trichology, so your technique is informed by understanding. After the intensive phase, Follicle Intelligence and mentoring support audit-backed improvement — so development continues beyond the course. This is beyond the short course: structured progression from learning to refinement, with support at every stage.
